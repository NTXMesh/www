📌 Project Context: Upgrading the Old System

This build serves as a major infrastructure upgrade. The original node setup inside the Bud Industries waterproof enclosure consisted strictly of a single battery and a 3D-printed radio tray.

To expand network resilience, the old battery is decommissioned to make physical space, while the original 3D-printed radio mounting tray is preserved. The upgrade re-integrates this tray to host two independent radios, an intelligent solar charge controller, and a high-capacity backup battery.

🔧 Step-by-Step Assembly Steps

1

Baseplate Installation & Battery Placement

  1. Install the structural base mounting plate inside the Bud Industries box using dedicated mounting standoffs and rubber vibration-isolation rings.
  2. Nest the new Meshnology 10,000mAh LiPo battery firmly into the bottom slot of the 3D-printed stack base tray to prevent physical shifting or impacts post-deployment.
2

Power Tray & Solar Charger Controller Installation

  1. Secure the middle 3D-printed power tray onto the standoffs directly above the battery layer using M3 screws.
  2. Mount the Adafruit BQ24074 Universal Solar Charger board to the power tray.
  3. Route the primary battery leads into a set of Wago 221 lever connectors to establish a tool-free, safe disconnect point before routing to the charger input.
⚠️ Critical Polarity Warning

Always use a multimeter to verify the positive (red, +) and negative (black, -) alignment on the battery's JST connector before clamping down the Wago levers. Certain lithium-polymer variants feature reversed polarity configurations that will instantaneously destroy the charger board and any downstream RAK wireless modules.

3

Stacking & Wiring the Dual WisBlock Nodes

  1. Mount the top tray containing both RAK Wireless WisBlock baseboards (one dedicated to Meshtastic, one to MeshCore) onto the standoff stack.
  2. Route the JST-PH output from the Adafruit charger's LOAD terminal directly to the battery input pins of the RAK baseboards.
  3. Connect Coaxial RF Pigtails:
    • Attach the Meshtastic (MT) node's U.FL connector to the bottom-left N-type bulkhead connector via an RG178 pigtail.
    • Attach the MeshCore (MC) node's U.FL connector directly to the top N-type bulkhead connector.
  4. Pass the positive and negative leads from the external solar panel through the bottom PG7/PG9 cable glands and wire them cleanly into the charger board's input terminals.
4

Pole Mounting & External Antenna Deployment

  1. Fasten the completed waterproof Bud Industries enclosure onto your permanent mounting mast using the heavy-duty UniFi pole mounts.
  2. Clamp the solar panel framework to the mast above or opposite the enclosure using the 3D-printed panel mount.
  3. Thread the McGill Microwave 6dBi fiberglass antenna onto the top N-connector port (dedicated to the MeshCore MC node).
  4. Thread the Alfa 5dBi whip antenna onto the bottom N-connector port (dedicated to the Meshtastic MT node).

🔌 Power & Wiring Logic

The node utilizes strict power path management to sustain 24/7 standalone operation:

1

Solar Generation & Smoothing

Power generated by the 5V 6W solar panel enters the enclosure through a sealed cable gland and terminates at the DCIN input of the Adafruit BQ24074 board. An inline Rubycon 4700uF capacitor buffers rapid fluctuations in solar intensity, ensuring smooth voltage tracking.

2

Intelligent Power Pathing

The charger's BATT port interacts with the 10,000mAh battery via the Wago distribution blocks. The BQ24074 automatically prioritizes running the system load while simultaneously channeling any excess overhead current to replenish the battery reserves.

3

Regulated System Load

The LOAD terminal of the charger outputs a steady, regulated 3.7V - 4.2V feed directly to the main RAK19007 baseboards via standard JST-PH connectors, bypassing volatile cell drop-off during transmission peaks.
